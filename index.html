Psychoacoustics Robustness : Exploring Audio Processing Robustness

Research Project, 2025


Abstract

Intelligent speech systems have been widely applied in various scenarios, but their core - speech understanding algorithms - can be misled by carefully constructed "small" noise to produce erroneous decisions, namely adversarial sample attacks. It can be proven that defense aims to provide a lower bound on accuracy for deep learning algorithms under a certain perturbation constraint. However, existing work has only established the relationship between model robustness and perturbation norms, and there is a mismatch between perturbation norms and human auditory perception. Attackers can find ways to hide adversarial perturbations outside of human senses. We propose a novel provable defense framework for speech understanding algorithms - the psychoacoustic oriented random smoothing framework. This paper verifies through rigorous mathematical proofs and extensive experiments that our framework can provide guarantees for the lower bound of model accuracy under specific audible constraints. The experiment verified that within the range of human auditory perception, the accuracy of the model under attack increased from 3.2% to no less than 69.3%. This article bridges the gap between provable defense and human auditory perception, establishing the relationship between model robustness and noise listenability.
